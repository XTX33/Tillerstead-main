Chapter 1

Understanding TCNA & ANSI Standards

The foundation of quality tile work starts with understanding industry standards that govern professional installations

What is TCNA?

The Tile Council of North America (TCNA) is the authoritative source for tile installation methods in North America. Their comprehensive handbook provides detailed, tested installation guidelines that professional contractors follow to ensure long-lasting, code-compliant installations.

üìò Key TCNA Resources
  • TCNA Handbook: Detailed installation method specifications with diagrams
  • Method EJ171: Standard shower installation over concrete or wood framing
  • Method B421: Waterproof membrane shower systems (bonded)
  • Method B422: Shower receptors with clamping-type drains
  • Technical Bulletins: Material compatibility and troubleshooting guidance

Vetting New Jersey Contractors

New Jersey has specific contractor licensing requirements‚Äîhere's how to verify compliance and protect yourself

NJ Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) License

In New Jersey, any contractor performing work over $500 MUST be registered with the Division of Consumer Affairs and hold an active HIC license. This is not optional‚Äîit's the law.

‚úì Verification Checklist
  • Verify HIC Registration: Visit nj.gov/consumeraffairs/hic and search the contractor's name or license number
  • Check License Status: Ensure the license is active (not suspended, revoked, or expired)
  • Verify Insurance: Contractor must carry liability insurance (minimum $500,000 recommended for tile work)
  • Review Complaint History: Check for filed complaints with Consumer Affairs
  • Confirm Business Address: Legitimate contractors have verifiable physical business locations
  • Check References: Ask for 3-5 recent similar projects with contact information

10 Essential Questions to Ask
