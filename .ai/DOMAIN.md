## TCNA & ANSI COMPLIANCE (2024-2025)

### Key Installation Standards
- **ANSI A108.01-2024**: General installation
- **ANSI A108.02-2024**: Portland cement mortar
- **ANSI A108.5-2024**: Thinset methods
- **ANSI A108.10-2024**: Wet area installations
- **ANSI A108.13-2024**: Waterproofing membranes
- **ANSI A108.19-2024**: Gauged porcelain panels

### Material Standards
- **ANSI A118.10-2024**: Waterproofing membranes
- **ANSI A118.15-2024**: Improved modified thinset
- **ANSI A137.1-2024**: Ceramic tile
- **ANSI A137.3-2024**: Gauged porcelain panels

### 2024 Critical Updates

#### Large Format Tile (LFT)
- **Definition**: ≥15" on any side (TCNA 2024)
- **Coverage**: 95% thinset (was 80%)
- **Thinset**: Modified, ISO 13007 C2 or better
- **Back-buttering**: Mandatory for >24"
- **Flatness**: 1/8" in 10' (rectified)

#### Gauged Porcelain Tile Panels (GPTP)
- **Thickness**: 3–6mm
- **Size**: Up to 5'×10'
- **Setting**: ANSI A118.15 (polymer-modified)
- **Trowel**: 1/2" × 1/2" min.

#### Waterproofing
- **ANSI A118.10**: Required for bonded membranes
- **Crack isolation**: Recommended for all floors
- **Types**: Sheet (e.g., KERDI), liquid (e.g., RedGard, Hydroban)
- **Cure**: 24–72 hours (per product)

#### Substrate Prep
- **Deflection**: L/360 max (floors)
- **Concrete**: 28-day cure min.
- **CBU**: Required in wet areas
- **Plywood**: 1-1/8" min. (exterior-grade)

## LARGE FORMAT TILE (LFT) INSTALLATION

- **Definition**: ≥15" side (TCNA 2024)
- **Flatness**: 1/8" in 10' (rectified), 1/4" in 10' (non-rectified)
- **Lippage**: Max 1/32" (rectified), 1/16" (cushioned)

### TCNA-Approved Method

1. **Thinset Coverage**: 95% min. (pull test)
2. **Back-buttering**: Mandatory >24"
3. **Lippage Control**: T-Lock, Raimondi, or equivalent
4. **Trowel Size**:
    | Tile Size         | Trowel Size                |
    |-------------------|----------------------------|
    | 12"–18"           | 1/2" × 1/2" square notch   |
    | 18"–24"/36"       | 3/4" × 3/4" square notch   |
    | 24"–48"+          | 1/2" U-notch or 3/4" sq.   |

## CARPENTRY FOR TILE

- **Joist spacing**: 16" o.c. max
- **Subfloor**: 3/4" exterior plywood min.
- **Second layer**: 1/2" CBU or 3/8"–1/2" plywood (total 1-1/8" min.)
- **Screw pattern**: 6" on joists, 8" in field
- **Deflection**: L/360 max

### Showers
- **Walls**: 2×4 studs 16" o.c. (2×6 for plumbing)
- **Blocking**: At 72" (showerhead), grab bars, shelves
- **Curb**: 2×6 min., sloped 1/4"/ft
- **Pre-slope**: 1/4"/ft to drain

### Niches
- **Recessed**: Frame between studs, slope bottom
- **Prefab**: KERDI-BOARD-SN, Wedi Fundo Riolito

### Vanities/Backsplashes
- **Vanity tops**: 3/4" plywood + 1/2" CBU/mortar bed
- **Backsplash**: 1/2" CBU in wet areas; drywall OK in dry with primer

## NJ HOME IMPROVEMENT CONTRACTOR (HIC) COMPLIANCE

- **License**: 13VH10808800 (Tillerstead LLC)
- **Scope**: Carpentry, tile, waterproofing (statewide NJ)
- **Display**: License on all contracts, estimates, ads

### NJ Consumer Fraud Act (N.J.S.A. 56:8-136)

**Contracts must include**:
1. Contractor name, address, phone, HIC #
2. Detailed work description
3. Itemized costs (materials, labor, permits)
4. Start/completion dates
5. Payment schedule (tied to progress)
6. 3-day right of rescission (bold, 12pt+)
7. Change order process
8. Warranty info

**Payment limits**:
- **Deposit**: Max 10% or $1,000 (lesser)
- **Milestone payments**: Only after progress
- **Final payment**: 10% held until substantial completion
- **Penalties**: Up to $10,000/violation + treble damages

**Insurance**:
- $1M liability min.
- Workers Comp if employees
- COI provided on request

### Permits & Inspections

- **Required**: Structural, plumbing, electrical, major remodels
- **Not required**: Tile replacement (no structural/plumbing), cosmetic updates
- **Always verify**: Local building department

**Codes**: 2018 IBC/IRC, 2018 UPC, IRC P2709 (showers)

## INDUSTRY TERMINOLOGY

- **Back-buttering**: Thinset on tile back
- **Beating in**: Compressing tile into thinset
- **Bond coat**: Thinset layer between substrate/tile
- **Comb/rake**: Notched trowel ridges
- **Efflorescence**: White salt deposits
- **Lippage**: Uneven tile height
- **Open time**: Thinset workability window
- **Plane**: Flatness of surface
- **Scratch coat**: First mortar layer in mud bed
- **Slake**: Resting mixed mortar before use
- **Wet-out**: Thinset penetration into tile

**Abbreviations**:  
- **CBU**: Cement Backer Unit  
- **DITRA**: Schluter uncoupling membrane  
- **KERDI**: Schluter waterproofing  
- **LFT**: Large Format Tile  
- **GPTP**: Gauged Porcelain Tile Panels  
- **SLC**: Self-Leveling Cement

## RED FLAGS & COMMON ISSUES

- **Thinset coverage <95%**: Hollow spots, failure
- **Wrong thinset**: Delamination (esp. porcelain)
- **Poor substrate prep**: Deflection, fastener issues
- **Unsealed grout**: Staining, leaks
- **Inadequate waterproofing**: Mold, rot, structural risk
- **Ignoring deflection**: Cracked grout, tile tenting

**NJ-specific**:
- **Hurricane zone**: Window flashing critical
- **Freeze-thaw**: Exterior tile must be frost-proof (<0.5% absorption)
- **Humidity**: Code-required ventilation (exhaust fans)
- **Older homes**: Lead/asbestos testing may be required
